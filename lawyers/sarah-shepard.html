About Sarah S. Shepard

Sarah S. Shepard is a highly regarded estate planning and probate attorney serving Huntsville, Alabama, and families throughout the North Alabama region. With more than 12 years of focused experience in estate planning, probate administration, trust creation, and elder law, Sarah has helped hundreds of individuals and families in Madison County protect their assets, plan for the future, and navigate the legal complexities that arise after the loss of a loved one. Her thoughtful, detail-oriented approach to estate planning has made her one of the most trusted attorneys in Huntsville for matters involving wills, trusts, and estate administration.

Huntsville's population has grown rapidly, and with that growth has come a diverse and increasingly affluent community of professionals, business owners, military families, and retirees who need sophisticated estate planning solutions. Whether a client is a young Huntsville professional creating their first will, a business owner structuring asset protection strategies, a military family stationed at Redstone Arsenal planning for unique deployment-related contingencies, or a retiree in the Monte Sano area seeking to establish a comprehensive trust, Sarah Shepard brings the same meticulous care and personalized attention to every engagement. She understands that estate planning is not a one-size-fits-all endeavor and works closely with each client to develop a plan tailored to their specific circumstances and goals.

Beyond her estate planning work, Sarah is one of Madison County's most experienced probate practitioners. When a loved one passes away, the survivors are often overwhelmed by grief and unfamiliar with the legal requirements for administering an estate in Alabama. Sarah guides families through the entire probate process in the Madison County Probate Court, from opening the estate and inventorying assets to addressing creditor claims, filing tax returns, and distributing inheritances. Her calm, empathetic demeanor and thorough knowledge of Alabama probate law provide a source of stability for families during an incredibly difficult time. Clients consistently describe her as patient, approachable, and exceptionally competent.

Practice Areas

Sarah S. Shepard focuses her practice on estate planning and probate law, offering comprehensive services that address the full lifecycle of wealth preservation and transfer for North Alabama families:

  • Wills: Drafting, reviewing, and updating last wills and testaments for Huntsville individuals and families, including simple wills, pour-over wills, and wills with testamentary trusts, all in compliance with Alabama statutory requirements.
  • Trusts: Creation of revocable living trusts, irrevocable trusts, special needs trusts, charitable trusts, and other trust vehicles designed to accomplish specific asset protection and estate planning objectives for families in Madison County and beyond.
  • Probate Administration: Full-service probate representation in the Madison County Probate Court, including estate opening, personal representative appointment, asset inventory, creditor notification, estate accounting, and final distribution to beneficiaries.
  • Powers of Attorney: Drafting durable financial powers of attorney and healthcare powers of attorney to ensure that trusted individuals can make critical decisions on a client's behalf in the event of incapacity.
  • Advance Healthcare Directives: Living wills and healthcare directives that clearly communicate a client's medical treatment preferences in accordance with Alabama law.
  • Elder Law: Legal counsel for seniors and their families in the Huntsville area on matters including Medicaid planning, long-term care planning, guardianship and conservatorship proceedings, and protection against financial exploitation.
  • Estate & Trust Litigation: Representation in will contests, trust disputes, breach of fiduciary duty claims, and other estate-related litigation in Madison County courts.

Case Results

Sarah S. Shepard has achieved meaningful outcomes for families and individuals across a broad range of estate planning and probate matters in Huntsville and Madison County. While each situation is unique, the following examples illustrate the scope of her work:

  • Designed and implemented a comprehensive estate plan for a Huntsville business owner that included a revocable living trust, buy-sell agreement coordination, and a succession plan that allowed the business to transition smoothly to the next generation while minimizing estate tax exposure.
  • Successfully administered a complex probate estate in Madison County Probate Court involving real property in multiple Alabama counties, significant investment portfolios, and competing claims from beneficiaries, completing the process efficiently and with full accounting transparency.
